# Playbook — Launching a £0 Lifestyle Business in Mid-2026

_Synthesised from 26 sources fetched and verified in this session (see Sources). Where a claim isn't backed by a fetched source, it is labelled **[first principles]** so a reader can audit which parts are evidence vs. reasoning._

## 1. Thesis: the lay of the land in May 2026

The £0-lifestyle-business space in mid-2026 looks structurally different from the 2018–2022 era that produced most of the canonical advice. Three things are true at once.

**First: AI has compressed execution to near-zero on the inputs solo founders used to compete on, and inflated the cost of being undifferentiated.** Dan Koe's framing is the cleanest: "the barrier of entry is lower, but the skill cap is higher" ([Source 6](https://letters.thedankoe.com/p/how-to-start-a-one-person-business)). Anyone can spin up a passable landing page, customer avatar, or chatbot in an afternoon. The market punishes anything that smells generic because a model can replicate it. The defensible work has moved up the stack: into taste, distribution, proprietary data, and embodied expertise that doesn't exist on the internet yet.

**Second: the people with money to spend are shifting.** Daniel Priestley's "plumbers will earn more than lawyers" thesis ([Source 1](https://www.shortform.com/podcast/episode/the-diary-of-a-ceo-with-steven-bartlett-2026-03-16-episode-summary-daniel-priestley-plumbers-will-earn-more-than-lawyers-i-predicted-2008-now-i-m-warning-about-2029), [Source 7](https://fortune.com/2026/03/19/plumbers-outearning-lawyers-daniel-priestley-blue-collar-vs-white-collar-american-dream/)) is the headline, but the operational read is more interesting: entry-level white-collar hiring has fallen 50% since 2019, blue-collar trades are projected to grow above the 4% average through 2033, and 30%+ of US workers will see at least 50% of tasks disrupted. That means two populations have new disposable income or new pain: rising trades (cash-rich, software-poor) and disrupted white-collar professionals (skill-rich, time-poor, anxious). Both are underserved by the "personal brand + course" template that dominated 2020–2023.

**Third: the channels that worked are bifurcating.** Aggregation-theory dynamics ([Source 20](https://stratechery.com/2015/aggregation-theory/)) still favour platforms that own the user relationship — but indie operators are now reporting CAC of £20–£80 with active social presences vs. £200–£600 for venture-funded competitors using paid acquisition ([Source 26](https://monolit.sh/blog/why-indie-hackers-are-winning-against-funded-startups-2026)). The trade is: if you publish consistently, distribution is free; if you don't, it's prohibitive. There's no middle.

### Five claims I'm willing to be wrong about (so this thesis is falsifiable)

1. **A £0 generic-AI-wrapper business launched in May 2026 will not reach £1k MRR within 12 months.** "Thin wrappers" are dead as a category; 90% of them fail by 2026 with most generating zero revenue ([Source 23](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/vertical-ai-micro-saas-business-model-2026)). I'll consider this claim broken if I find three counter-examples that launched after Jan 2026, have no proprietary data, and crossed £1k MRR.
2. **A solo operator with no audience, starting today, will reach the first £100 of revenue faster via a productised service than via a SaaS product.** Welsh, Koe, and Czerny's pattern (service → productise → SaaS) ([Sources 4](https://www.justinwelsh.me/), [5](https://letters.thedankoe.com/p/how-id-build-a-one-person-business), [24](https://www.somethingsblog.com/2026/01/24/real-indie-hacker-success-stories-that-prove-its-still-possible-in-2026/)) suggests this; broken if a SaaS with no audience hits paid customers in week 1 of launch routinely.
3. **A daily-publishing cadence on one owned channel for 90 days, with cohort feedback, will outperform a thrice-weekly cadence across three rented channels for the same period.** Atomic-essay/build-in-public evidence ([Sources 22](https://www.ship30for30.com/post/how-to-write-an-atomic-essay-a-beginners-guide), [14](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/from-0-to-1k-mrr-in-8-months-bootstrapping-habit-pixel-as-a-solo-dev-684b6c056d)) supports the cadence; the *one channel* part is my call — diluted multi-platform is the consistent failure mode.
4. **Within the next 12 months, AI-overview search results will continue to suppress generic top-of-funnel SEO content (how-to-X for broad query), but will not meaningfully suppress branded/community-led search.** Implication: don't bet £0 on long-tail SEO blogs as a primary acquisition channel. Broken if Google ships a change that resurrects long-tail informational SEO.
5. **A solo operator with £0 in 2026 cannot win on advice categories where the operator has no track record.** "How to make money online" / "productivity for founders" / "build a personal brand" categories are now noise floors. Wedge has to be either a) genuine domain expertise the operator already has, or b) a public learning journey ("here is me, going from 0 to N") — not synthesised hot takes ([Sources 16](https://nocodeexits.substack.com/p/greg-isenbergs-playbook-for-community), [22](https://www.ship30for30.com/post/how-to-write-an-atomic-essay-a-beginners-guide)).

### The shape of the play this implies

A £0 lifestyle business launched in mid-2026 will likely look like this: **one operator + a narrow, embodied wedge (a job-to-be-done they understand from the inside) + a single owned distribution channel + a productised offer that converts attention into recurring or repeat revenue, with vertical-specific defensibility (data, workflow, taste, or community) bolted on as it proves out.** It will *not* look like: a generic newsletter about productivity, a course teaching how to build a business, an AI wrapper for a generic task, or a "personal brand" with no underlying offer.

This is the bet the playbook is calibrated for.

## 2. Frameworks library

Each framework: name → one-sentence definition → how I'll apply it (operator-facing) → source.

### Priestley-orbit frameworks

**KPI / 5 Ps — Pitch · Publish · Product · Profile · Partnership.** Priestley's five-step path to becoming a "key person of influence" in a micro-niche.
- *How I'll apply it:* Use as the macro sequencing model. **Pitch** = one crisp sentence I can repeat ("I help X get Y by doing Z"). **Publish** = the daily/weekly cadence on one owned channel — non-negotiable. **Product** = the first paid offer at ≤£100 to test pull. **Profile** = the bio, the site, the byline-stack — built last, not first. **Partnership** = post-traction. The order matters: skipping Publish to chase Profile is the single most common solopreneur fail mode. Toby Sinclair's distillation reinforces it: *"Publishing creates credibility; products create cash"* ([Source 2](https://www.tobysinclair.com/post/summary-key-person-of-influence-by-daniel-priestley)).

**Oversubscribed / Campaign-Driven Enterprise.** Engineer demand to exceed supply *before* selling: Determine Supply → Create Demand → Word-of-Mouth → Sales → Delivery & Iteration ([Source 3](https://www.shortform.com/summary/oversubscribed-summary-daniel-priestley)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Pre-launch every paid offer with a campaign — a waitlist, a countdown, a cohort cap. Never run a perpetual "buy whenever" page until I've sold out one closed cohort first. Priestley's nudge to act as a **campaign manager not a salesperson** is the operating mode: I'm not closing one customer at a time, I'm running events that have demand peaks.

**The Pendulum — blue-collar ascendancy / white-collar disruption.** AI commoditises digitised cognitive work first; embodied/local/relational work is structurally protected ([Sources 1](https://www.shortform.com/podcast/episode/the-diary-of-a-ceo-with-steven-bartlett-2026-03-16-episode-summary-daniel-priestley-plumbers-will-earn-more-than-lawyers-i-predicted-2008-now-i-m-warning-about-2029), [7](https://fortune.com/2026/03/19/plumbers-outearning-lawyers-daniel-priestley-blue-collar-vs-white-collar-american-dream/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Two implications. (a) Buyer side: trades/services-businesses owners are increasingly cash-flush and software-light — they're an under-served B2B audience. (b) Operator side: anything I build should either ride embodiment (taste, presence, on-the-record human expertise) or solve an actual workflow that won't be auto-eaten by a frontier model in 18 months. Run the **tombstone test**: write the epitaph for this business in 2028 — if "model release made it obsolete" is the most likely cause of death, kill the idea now.

**Vitality / human-presence-as-foundation.** Priestley's pre-condition for the 5 Ps: be the kind of person who listens, builds empathy, connects and trusts easily ([Source 2](https://www.tobysinclair.com/post/summary-key-person-of-influence-by-daniel-priestley)).
- *How I'll apply it:* This is the cheapest, most-ignored framework on the list. In a market where AI-generated everything is plentiful, the operator's voice/face/specifics-of-life are the moat. Implication: any business plan that requires hiding the operator is the wrong one for this brief.

### Outside-the-Priestley-orbit frameworks

**The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick).** Three rules of customer interviews: talk about their life, not your idea; ask about specifics in the past not hypotheticals; talk less, listen more. Beware compliments, fluff, and feature requests as the three categories of "bad data" ([Source 9](https://mtlynch.io/book-reports/the-mom-test/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Every "audience research" message I send asks for a past-tense story ("the last time you tried to do X, what happened?") and never asks "would you pay for X?" Pricing is inferred from what they currently spend on alternatives, not from what they say they'd be willing to pay.

**Jobs-To-Be-Done (Christensen, milkshake).** Customers don't buy products; they *hire* them to do a job. The competition is anything else that could do the job ([Source 13](https://www.fullstory.com/blog/clayton-christensen-jobs-to-be-done-framework-product-development/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* For any offer, I'll write the JTBD statement: "When [situation], [persona] wants to [motivation], so they can [outcome]." The "anti-competitor" exercise — what *else* are they hiring instead? — is the fastest way to find pricing anchors and positioning angles I'd otherwise miss.

**Hook Model (Nir Eyal).** Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment, looping ([Source 10](https://www.nirandfar.com/how-to-manufacture-desire/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Mostly as a *diagnostic*, not a builder. If the offer has no internal trigger (no emotion that pulls users back), no easy action, no variability in reward, and no investment that compounds — it's a one-time transaction, not a business. For a £0 lifestyle business, I'd rather know that early.

**Traction / Bullseye Framework + the 50% rule (Weinberg & Mares).** 19 channels; brainstorm across all, pick 3 for the inner ring, run cheap tests, double down on the winner. Spend 50% of time on traction, not just product ([Source 11](https://www.nateliason.com/notes/traction-gabriel-weinberg-justin-mares)).
- *How I'll apply it:* The 50% rule is the killer. Most solo founders sink 95% into product. I'll cap product work and force traction work in the same week, every week. For inner-ring channels at £0: Content Marketing, Community Building, Engineering as Marketing (free tools), Speaking/podcasts, Existing Platforms (LinkedIn, X, niche subreddits). Outer-ring rejected by budget: SEM, Social Ads, Offline Ads.

**Live in the Future + Schlep Blindness + the Well Criterion (Paul Graham).** Don't think up ideas — *notice* them by living at the leading edge. Stripe-style: the biggest ideas hide behind the work people unconsciously won't do. Build "something a small number of people want a lot" not "something many people want a little" ([Source 12](http://paulgraham.com/startupideas.html)).
- *How I'll apply it:* The well criterion is the wedge filter. The schlep test is the moat filter. "Who wants this so much they'll use it even when it's a crappy v1 made by a one-person team they've never heard of?" — if I can't name three named humans, the idea isn't well-formed.

**Aggregation Theory (Ben Thompson).** On the modern internet, distribution is free; the entities that own the *user relationship* extract the value, while suppliers commoditise ([Source 20](https://stratechery.com/2015/aggregation-theory/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Operationally: every rented channel (LinkedIn, X, Substack, YouTube, etc.) is a recruiting ground, not a home. The *home* is email-on-my-domain plus, eventually, a community I host. The KPI for any platform investment is "subscribers converted to my list", not "followers on the platform".

**PARA + CODE (Tiago Forte).** PARA: organise everything by Project / Area / Resource / Archive. CODE: Capture → Organize → Distill → Express. Ship work as "Intermediate Packets" instead of monolithic projects ([Source 19](https://fortelabs.com/blog/basboverview/)).
- *How I'll apply it:* This is the operator-OS layer. Every research file in this repo *is* an intermediate packet — a unit that can be shipped, referenced, or composed into a larger one. No giant unfinished docs.

**Atomic Essay (Cole & Bush, Ship 30 for 30).** 250 words, daily, one idea, designed to test resonance rather than perfect a thesis. "You can't steer a stationary ship" ([Source 22](https://www.ship30for30.com/post/how-to-write-an-atomic-essay-a-beginners-guide)).
- *How I'll apply it:* The 250-word constraint is the gift. Apply as the daily atom, then assemble weekly longer pieces (Clouse's flywheel — see below) from the highest-resonance atoms. Don't write the long piece first.

**ACP — Audience → Community → Product (Greg Isenberg).** Build audience first, gate community behind it, ship product into the warm community last ([Source 16](https://nocodeexits.substack.com/p/greg-isenbergs-playbook-for-community)). "Products can be copied, but communities can't."
- *How I'll apply it:* Inverts the SaaS-default "build then sell". For £0, this is the only sane ordering: I have zero capital but unlimited compounding time on a public output. Audience is the asset that lets the product not require ads. Isenberg's 2026 nudge — *superniche*, AI-leveraged operations, the "least sexy problems have the best unit economics" — is the niche-picking complement to PG's well criterion.

**Audience Growth Flywheel (Jay Clouse).** Weekly long-form essay → social amplification → newsletter subscriptions → automated welcome sequence collecting pain → essay topics fed by pain → repeat. 7,500 → 50,000 in two years on this loop ([Source 18](https://www.kingsmaker.co/blog/unlocking-the-content-flywheel-how-jay-clouse-grew-his-newsletter-7x-in-two-years)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Combine with atomic essays: 5–7 atoms per week → assembled long-form on Saturday → social spokes from that → subscriber-pain-driven editorial loop. Single closed system; no other content surface gets work until this one is stable.

**Vertical-AI thesis / 10-criterion scoring (aimagicx, 2026).** Generic wrappers dead; defensibility comes from owned workflow + proprietary data + embedded domain expertise. Solo operators in vertical AI hitting ~$30k MRR by month 12 with 85% gross margin ([Source 23](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/vertical-ai-micro-saas-business-model-2026)).
- *How I'll apply it:* If the chosen idea ends up being software-shaped, this is the screen. If it's a media/service shape, this still informs the *moat*: where's the proprietary data going to come from, and what's the workflow only I would build?

**Build-in-Public Funnel (Habit Pixel / IH 2026 case).** Documented operator: launch on app stores + Reddit + X; tedious early growth; "build in public" on X around month 6 = inflection point; PPP pricing + localization unlocked international markets; Black Friday + New Year seasonality = compounding revenue events; $28 → $1,000+ MRR in 8 months ([Source 14](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/from-0-to-1k-mrr-in-8-months-bootstrapping-habit-pixel-as-a-solo-dev-684b6c056d)).
- *How I'll apply it:* This is the *concrete* tactical recipe, not a generalisable framework — but it's the closest thing to a runnable 2025–2026 template I've seen. Use as the implementation reference for any v1 paid offer.

**Lifestyle Filter [first principles, informed by Welsh/Koe/Levels/Abdaal].** Before locking in any concept, ask: at success, what does my Tuesday look like? If success requires me to be on Zoom 6 hours/day, run a team, or chase enterprise sales — it's not a lifestyle business by definition. Welsh: ~4 hours/day, kids, exercise, reading ([Source 4](https://www.justinwelsh.me/)). Levels: rough and fast, cash-controls-destiny ([Source 8](https://levels.io/nomad-list-founder/)). Abdaal: 18-person team — *no longer a lifestyle business by Welsh's standard*, useful as a contrast ([Source 17](https://www.microempires.cc/p/ali-abdaal)).
- *How I'll apply it:* Hard veto on any concept that requires more than ~25 hours/week at steady-state to keep alive. Marked **[first principles]** because no single fetched source defines the filter explicitly — it's a synthesis from operator profiles.

That's 4 Priestley-orbit + 12 outside = 16 frameworks.

## 3. Scoring rubric (this is what I'll use in Phase 1's `decision.md`)

Each candidate concept is scored against nine weighted criteria, total = 100. I'll keep the rubric runnable: each criterion has a 0/4/7/10 scale (kill / weak / acceptable / strong) so I can score in minutes, not days.

| # | Criterion | Weight | What I'm checking |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Wedge clarity** | 15 | Can I name *three actual humans* who'll buy v1 (with crap branding, no track record) and where I find them? (PG well criterion + Mom Test) |
| 2 | **Operator-fit** | 15 | Does this require expertise/taste/contacts/temperament I either have or can plausibly build in evenings? (Lifestyle filter + Priestley vitality) |
| 3 | **£0 distribution path** | 15 | Is there a free channel where the target already congregates, and a non-spammy way I can show up there 4× per week? (Traction inner-ring) |
| 4 | **AI-survival (tombstone test)** | 12 | Write the obit in 2028: "Killed by [X]." If X is "next OpenAI release" or "Claude Sonnet 5", score 0. (Pendulum + Vertical AI thesis) |
| 5 | **Owned vs rented revenue** | 10 | At steady state, does revenue flow through my list/site (owned) or only via a platform (rented)? (Aggregation Theory) |
| 6 | **Compound asset** | 10 | Does *every week* of work make next week easier (audience, SEO, data, community), or does it reset? (Clouse flywheel + ACP) |
| 7 | **Speed to first £100** | 8 | Can I plausibly take £100 from a stranger within 30 days of starting, with a v0 offer? (Welsh/Koe service-first pattern) |
| 8 | **Lifestyle filter** | 10 | At success, is my Tuesday at ≤25 hrs/week and free of meetings I dread? (Lifestyle filter) |
| 9 | **Reversibility** | 5 | If it fails in 6 months, what's the blast radius? (£10 domain + reputation = low; £0 + my name = lower) |

**Pass threshold: 60/100. Kill threshold: below 50, or score 0 on any of criteria 1, 2, 3, or 4.**

### Worked example: "Generic AI Prompt Pack on Gumroad"

A throwaway concept to prove the rubric is runnable: "I sell £19 packs of curated prompts for marketers."

| # | Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|---|-----------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Wedge clarity | 4 | I can name a category of buyer ("marketers") but no three specific humans. |
| 2 | Operator-fit | 4 | I'm not a marketer; my expertise is generic. |
| 3 | £0 distribution path | 7 | LinkedIn + X work for this audience for free. |
| 4 | AI-survival (tombstone) | 0 | Obit: "Killed by OpenAI native prompt library + Claude Skills." **Auto-kill.** |
| 5 | Owned vs rented | 7 | Gumroad is rented but emails are mine. |
| 6 | Compound asset | 4 | Each pack is a SKU; no audience compounding unless I also publish. |
| 7 | Speed to first £100 | 10 | Could plausibly do it in week 1 with one viral post. |
| 8 | Lifestyle filter | 7 | Tuesday is fine — just refreshing SKUs. |
| 9 | Reversibility | 5 | Failure costs me £10 and nothing else. |

**Total: 48/100, plus a 0 on criterion 4 → killed.**

The rubric works: it catches that even though the speed-to-revenue is great, the tombstone-test failure makes it a non-starter. That's exactly the kind of false positive I want the rubric to surface.

## 4. Contradictions resolved (≥5 required; I have 7)

These are pairs of credible advice from people in this brief that *genuinely conflict*. My call on each, with the reasoning.

**Contradiction 1: "Niche down brutally" (Welsh, Koe, Steph Smith, Isenberg's superniche) vs. "Write what you'd talk about for an hour" (Clouse audience growth, Cole atomic essays).**
- *My call:* Niche *the offer*; broaden *the publishing voice*. Concrete: pick a narrow paying customer (e.g. "freelance UK accountants with 3–15 clients") for the product, but write across the operator's actual obsessions for the first 90 days to find which threads pull strangers in. Niche emerges from publishing, not from a planning doc.
- *Why:* For a £0 operator with no audience, the discovery loop *is* the niche-finding mechanism. Premature niching with no signal is the same mistake as the cold-start product launch the Mom Test exists to prevent.

**Contradiction 2: "Community-first" (Isenberg) vs. "Newsletter-first" (Welsh, Smith, Clouse, Parr).**
- *My call:* Newsletter is the asset, community is the moat. Build the newsletter for 6 months, *then* gate a community behind a small paid tier. Don't start the community on day 1.
- *Why:* Community-first works at >£0 because you need critical mass to make a community feel alive. At £0 / zero audience, a community of 6 lurkers is a graveyard. The newsletter is the recruitment funnel that makes the community viable when it launches. Sam Parr's Hampton came *after* The Hustle ([Source 21](https://theygotacquired.com/podcast/sam-parr-the-hustle/)).

**Contradiction 3: "Aggregation theory says don't depend on platforms" (Thompson) vs. "Welsh's hub-and-spoke distributes across LinkedIn/X/Instagram first".**
- *My call:* Platforms are recruitment, email-on-domain is retention. Welsh's hub-and-spoke is correct *operationally* but only works because the spokes funnel into the hub (his email list). Treat every platform follower as a rented relationship; every email subscriber as owned.
- *Why:* The aggregation-theory worry isn't "don't use platforms" — it's "don't let platforms own your customer relationship." A LinkedIn algorithm change can zero a personal brand overnight; an email list can't (barring deliverability collapse, which is its own problem).

**Contradiction 4: "Build in public from day 1" (Levels, Habit Pixel case) vs. "Don't pitch — ask Mom Test questions" (Fitzpatrick).**
- *My call:* Mom Test in 1:1 conversations, build-in-public in broadcast. Never blur. The Mom Test rules apply when you're trying to *learn*; build-in-public is when you're trying to *be discovered*. Doing build-in-public during an interview pollutes the data; doing Mom Test during a launch wastes the audience moment.
- *Why:* Same mouth, two modes. Confusing them is why so many "launches" produce only compliments (the worst kind of bad data per Fitzpatrick).

**Contradiction 5: "Daily atomic essays" (Cole/Bush) vs. "Weekly long-form essay" (Clouse) vs. "2–4 hours/day deep work" (Koe).**
- *My call:* Stack them. Daily atoms (250 words, the discovery layer) → assemble best atoms into Saturday long-form (the retention layer) → social spokes Sunday–Wednesday seeded from the long-form. Total ~1 hour/day on writing, not 4. Koe's "skill cap is higher" is real, but for the *publishing layer specifically*, more time ≠ better; cadence + iteration beats craft.
- *Why:* The three sources are optimising for different goals (writing reps, retention loop, deep-work capacity). The conflict is artificial once you separate the goals.

**Contradiction 6: "Boring service businesses, real-world cash flow" (Codie Sanchez orbit) vs. "Digital/scalable, no overhead" (almost everyone else).**
- *My call:* For a £0 + evenings constraint, digital wins on the operator-fit and reversibility criteria. But borrow Sanchez's *insight*: the best opportunities are in underserved, "boring" workflows — and apply that lens to the digital play. The vertical-AI thesis ([Source 23](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/vertical-ai-micro-saas-business-model-2026)) is essentially "Sanchez's boring business" applied to software: HOA compliance, dental lab comms, freight broker ops are the digital equivalent of car washes.
- *Why:* Acquiring a real-world business requires capital and full-time attention — both vetoed by the brief. Sanchez's heuristic ("uncool problems with the best unit economics") is portable to the digital case.

**Contradiction 7: "Vertical AI / deep moat" (aimagicx) vs. "Personal brand / fast, light moat" (Welsh, Koe, Cole).**
- *My call:* Personal brand is the *wedge*, vertical product is the *destination*. Start with personal-brand publishing (£0, reversible, compounds) for 3–6 months while doing Mom-Test conversations inside one underserved vertical that the publishing happens to attract. Let the vertical product fall out of the conversations, not the other way round.
- *Why:* "Build vertical AI cold" requires domain expertise the operator either has or doesn't. "Build personal brand first" buys the time and the inbound conversations that *reveal* whether a vertical opportunity is real. Either ordering can work; this ordering is reversible and uses the constraints (£0, evenings) as a feature.

## 5. The 2026-specific edge — what's working *now* that wasn't in 2022

This is the section that ages fastest. Here's the read as of May 2026, with the receipts.

### Alive in 2026 (works *more* than it did in 2022)

- **Build-in-public on X with PPP pricing + localization.** Habit Pixel doubled MRR twice in 6 weeks by adding international pricing and 12 languages, after stagnating with English-only / US pricing for 6 months ([Source 14](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/from-0-to-1k-mrr-in-8-months-bootstrapping-habit-pixel-as-a-solo-dev-684b6c056d)).
- **Show HN > Product Hunt for technical solo launches.** Show HN's "show" tab is less competitive, posts dwell longer, and a frontpage HN spot now reliably drives 10K–50K visits vs. 3K–15K for a top PH launch ([Source 15](https://www.lucasfcosta.com/blog/hn-launch)).
- **Vertical-AI micro-SaaS with proprietary data/workflow.** Solo founders hitting $30k MRR by month 12 in the right vertical (vet practice mgmt, HOA compliance, freight broker ops, etc.) — a category that effectively didn't exist as a solo play in 2022 because the underlying LLMs weren't capable enough ([Source 23](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/vertical-ai-micro-saas-business-model-2026)).
- **Build-in-public + freemium + seasonal events (Black Friday, New Year).** Habit Pixel's largest revenue spikes were Black Friday lifetime deals + New Year resolution surge. Solo founders can now run "campaigns" (Priestley Oversubscribed) at zero cost via timing alone.
- **Newsletter-as-business with side-doors via organic search.** Clouse 7x'd to 50K subs partly because long-form essays rank on Google and have CTA "side doors" back to the list ([Source 18](https://www.kingsmaker.co/blog/unlocking-the-content-flywheel-how-jay-clouse-grew-his-newsletter-7x-in-two-years)). The AI-overview risk is real for *generic* SEO but evergreen branded-search/long-form essays are still indexed and clicked.
- **Indie hackers structurally beat funded peers on CAC.** £20–£80 vs. £200–£600 — the gap widened in 2025–2026 as paid acquisition costs rose and organic distribution stayed cheap for those who publish ([Source 26](https://monolit.sh/blog/why-indie-hackers-are-winning-against-funded-startups-2026)).

### Dead in 2026 (used to work, doesn't now)

- **Thin AI wrappers.** Margins compressed to 25–35% as API costs claim 40–70% of revenue and OpenAI absorbs the popular use-cases natively (custom GPTs, canvas, memory, file analysis) ([Sources 23](https://www.aimagicx.com/blog/vertical-ai-micro-saas-business-model-2026), [5](https://letters.thedankoe.com/p/how-id-build-a-one-person-business)).
- **Generic top-of-funnel SEO blogs.** AI Overviews eat the click for informational queries. Branded/educational *destinations* still work; "10 best [thing]" listicles do not.
- **Platform-only monetization (Instagram/X creator payouts) as primary revenue.** Pays $300–$1K/month vs. $50K–$200K+ from owned offers to the same audience ([Source 5](https://letters.thedankoe.com/p/how-id-build-a-one-person-business)).
- **Course-only products with no recurring layer.** Welsh's "high-ticket course" critique ([Source 25](https://www.justinwelsh.me/articles), title: *High-Ticket is (Mostly) a Sham*) tracks: one-off £997 courses without retention infrastructure are now closer to lottery than business.
- **"Agency for X" plays where X is something AI now does end-to-end.** Logo design, basic copywriting, transcription, basic data entry — agency margins collapsed.
- **Dropshipping / template SaaS / clone-Hot-App** — explicitly vetoed by the brief but worth noting they're worse 2026 bets than 2022 bets, not better.

### The asymmetric bet, given all of the above

Pair an **embodied, narrowly-niched wedge** (operator-as-the-product, vitality-as-moat) with **a productised offer that has a recurring or repeat-purchase layer**, distributed via **one owned channel built on daily atoms + weekly long-form**, with the *option* (not the obligation) of a vertical-AI product layer if the audience reveals a specific painful workflow. The wedge has to come from the operator's actual life — not from the operator's wish-list — because the cost of being undifferentiated in 2026 is now structurally higher than the cost of niching too hard.

This is what the Phase 1 `decision.md` will pick *for*.
