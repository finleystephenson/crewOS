# Cold-outreach: UK trades Facebook groups

## Target groups (operator joins one-time, in any order)

1. **On The Tools** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/onthetools/
2. **UK Plumbers, Heating & Gas Engineers** — search Facebook for this exact phrase
3. **Electricians UK** — search Facebook for this exact phrase
4. **UK Builders & Trades** — search Facebook for this exact phrase
5. **Self Employed Tradesman UK** — search Facebook for this exact phrase

Some require admin approval to join — apply and wait. Most approve within 24h. Joining is the only operator step; once in, I draft posts and you paste-and-publish in one ~10-min session.

## Posting rules (so we don't get banned)

- **Read the rules of each group** before posting. Most prohibit pure self-promotion. Most allow "share what you're building, especially if you're a tradesperson or work for tradespeople."
- **Post as a person, not a brand.** Even though you're posting under the Crew OS Company Page conceptually, Facebook needs you to post as a personal-account member of the group. Use your real name.
- **Don't post in more than 2 groups per day.** Facebook's spam classifier hits cross-posting hard.
- **Always respond to every comment** within 6 hours. If you can't, don't post.

## Post template (one variant — vary the opener for each group)

> Looking for 5 UK tradespeople to try a quoting tool I've built.
>
> Quick context: I'm 18, building this in public. The tool turns a voice note + photos from a site visit into a sent, branded quote in under 10 minutes, then auto-follows-up the customer.
>
> Free for 14 days, no card. Built specifically for 1–5 person crews — sparkies, plumbers, builders, gas engineers, groundworkers. Designed around UK paperwork (CIS, VAT, the bits ServiceTitan doesn't bother with at this scale).
>
> Not pitching to take your money — looking for honest feedback on what works and what doesn't. If it helps, you keep using it at £49/mo. If not, you walk away.
>
> Reply or DM if you want in. crewos.co.uk

### Variations to rotate

- **For On The Tools / general trades:** open with "Quick favour, looking for…"
- **For plumber/sparky-specific groups:** swap "sparkies, plumbers, builders, gas engineers, groundworkers" to lead with the group's trade.
- **For self-employed-focused groups:** lead with "If you've ever lost a Saturday night to writing quotes…"

## What to do when someone replies

- **Positive ("yes interested"):** reply within 4 hours with a one-liner — "Great, send me your email or DM it and I'll set you up tonight. No card." Forward the email/DM to me; I'll send the onboarding link.
- **Sceptical ("how is this different from X?"):** reply with one specific difference — "Honest answer: ServiceTitan is built for 20+ person crews and is £200+. This is built for 1–5 and is £49. The voice-note input is the actual novel thing." Don't oversell.
- **Hostile / dismissive:** thank them, move on. Don't argue.
- **Off-topic / spammy:** ignore.

## Cadence

- Week 2: post in groups 1 and 2 (Tue and Thu).
- Week 3: post in groups 3 and 4 (Tue and Thu).
- Week 4: post in group 5 + run a follow-up post in the most engaged group with a "here's what we learned from week 2" angle.

Total operator time: ~6 paste-and-publish actions × 2 min each = ~12 min across the 30 days.
